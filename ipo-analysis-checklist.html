IPO Analysis Checklist

Parameter Meaning & Where to Find Analysis for XYZ IPO
SECTION 1: BUSINESS FUNDAMENTALS
Business Model How the company generates revenue and profit.
(Offer Document section: "About our Business")
✅ Positive: Simple, understandable, and scalable business.
❌ Negative: Overly complex model that is difficult to understand.
Positive.ANSWER
Industry Growth The growth potential and health of the industry.
(Offer Document section: "Our Industry")
✅ Positive: Company is in a high-growth industry with strong future demand.
❌ Negative: Company is in a stagnant or declining industry.
Negative.ANSWER
Competitive Advantage (Moat) The company's unique strengths that protect it from competitors.
(Offer Document section: "Our Competitive Strengths")
✅ Positive: Strong brand, patents, unique technology, large distribution network.
❌ Negative: No clear differentiator; operates in a highly commoditized market.
Mixed.ANSWER
SECTION 2: FINANCIAL HEALTH & PERFORMANCE
Revenue & Profit Growth The year-on-year increase in sales and net profits.
(Financial Statements in Offer Document)
✅ Positive: Consistent, strong growth (>15-20%) over the last 3-5 years. Profit growth is faster than revenue growth.
❌ Negative: Stagnant, declining, or "profitless" growth.
Positive.ANSWER
Profit Margins The percentage of revenue that turns into profit (EBITDA Margin for InvITs).
(Financial Statements in Offer Document)
✅ Positive: Stable or increasing margins, higher than industry peers.
❌ Negative: Declining margins, indicating weakening pricing power or rising costs.
Positive.ANSWER
Debt-to-Equity Ratio The company's total debt relative to its shareholders' equity.
(Financial Statements in Offer Document)
✅ Positive: Low ratio (<1 is ideal), or a clear decreasing trend.
❌ Negative: High ratio (>2 can be risky) or rapidly increasing debt.
Positive.ANSWER
Return on Net Worth (RoNW) Measures profit generated from unitholders' money.
(KPIs in Offer Document)
✅ Positive: High and consistent RoNW (>15-20%).
❌ Negative: Low or declining RoNW, indicating inefficient use of equity.
Positive.ANSWER
Cash Flow from Operations Cash generated by the company's core business activities.
(Cash Flow Statement in Offer Document)
✅ Positive: Consistently positive and growing cash flow.
❌ Negative: Negative or erratic cash flow from operations.
Positive.ANSWER
SECTION 3: IPO DETAILS
Use of IPO Funds The company's reasons for raising money.
(Offer Document section: "Objects of the Issue")
✅ Positive: Funding business expansion (Capex) or R&D.
❌ Negative: Primarily to repay existing debt or for an "Offer for Sale" (OFS).
Positive.ANSWER
Offer for Sale (OFS) Portion of IPO where existing shareholders sell their shares.
✅ Positive: Small or zero OFS component.
❌ Negative: A large OFS, as it means promoters are cashing out.
Positive.ANSWER
SECTION 4: VALUATION
P/E & P/B Ratios Price-to-Earnings & Price-to-Book ratios.
(Compare with listed peers)
✅ Positive: Ratios are lower than or in line with industry competitors.
❌ Negative: Ratios are significantly higher than peers, suggesting the IPO is overpriced.
Positive.ANSWER
SECTION 5: MANAGEMENT & SHAREHOLDING
Promoter Holding (Post-IPO) Percentage of the company founders will own after the IPO.
(Offer Document section: "Shareholding Pattern")
✅ Positive: Promoters retain a significant majority stake (>50-60%).
❌ Negative: Promoter holding falls to a low level.
Positive.ANSWER
Anchor Investors Large institutional investors who invest before the IPO opens.
(Announced a day before the IPO opens)
✅ Positive: Reputable and well-known institutions are anchor investors.
❌ Negative: Lack of interest from strong anchor investors.
Positive.ANSWER
BONUS: MARKET SENTIMENT INDICATOR
Grey Market Premium (GMP) An unofficial premium at which IPO shares trade before listing.
(Found on IPO tracking websites)
✅ Positive: Strong positive GMP indicates high demand.
❌ Negative: Low or negative GMP indicates weak demand.
⚠️ Caution: GMP is unofficial and can be volatile. Do not rely on it solely.
Positive.ANSWER
